This paper proposes a Hybrid Safety Shield (HSS) for Connected and Autonomous Vehicles (CAVs) that ensures safety during lane changes by combining rule-based logic with Control Barrier Functions. We integrate HSS with Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) in a framework called MARL-HSS to improve traffic efficiency while maintaining safety. MARL-HSS is evaluated in an on-ramp merging scenario with different traffic densities, demonstrating strict compliance with safety constraints and stable learned policies that balance safety and efficiency better than a baseline controller.
