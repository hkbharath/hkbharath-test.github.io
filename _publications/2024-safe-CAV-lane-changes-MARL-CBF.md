This paper proposes a hybrid framework that integrates Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) with Control Barrier Functions (CBFs) to produce provably safe lane changes for Connected and Autonomous Vehicles (CAVs). The approach combines the learning flexibility of MARL policies with the formal safety guarantees of CBFs, evaluated in simulation on on-ramp merging and lane change scenarios.
